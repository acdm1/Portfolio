Abstract

Pierce County has an interesting history where noise and policy are considered within the same context. Could policy maps communicate themes pulled from environmental psychology, to better inform policy makers and readers as they attempt to recognize contemporary and future struggles around equity though? Pierce County serves here as the location for observing how noise is experienced based on a variety of social determinants. By considering the property values, geographic/scenic locations, and the ethnic identities of those occupying urban communities, we may learn more about ourselves, and thus better adapt to future calls for justice. The aim of this project was to create a visually and sonically fulfilling experience, encompassed within a multimedia map, which is accessible as both a recreational and educational tool.

Keywords: Noise, Policy, Geography, Environmental Psychology, American Studies, Health & Society, Community Engagement
